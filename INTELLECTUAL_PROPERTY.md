ZFG Studio's: Intellectual Property and Licensing Governance
This policy establishes the formal framework for the protection and distribution of intellectual property associated with ZFG Studio's. All development assets, software architectures, and documentation are categorized to ensure maximum legal protection while maintaining industry-standard practices.

Proprietary Commercial Assets
All game experiences, including but not limited to, Merge Pets Simulator and Chaos: Re, are strictly proprietary. These assets function under a closed-source model to maintain market exclusivity, platform security, and the integrity of our intellectual property.

All content, including source code, game mechanics, visual assets, branding, and intellectual property associated with ZFG Studio's, is the exclusive property of ZFG Studio's. Unauthorized replication, distribution, or decomplication of this experience constitutes a violation of international copyright laws. ZFG Studio's reserves all rights to pursue legal, administrative, and platform-level remedies against any unauthorized usage.

Open-Source Utility Contributions
For specialized utility code, administrative frameworks, or modules intended for public developer contribution, ZFG Studio's employs the MIT License. This approach grants the developer community usage rights while simultaneously providing ZFG Studio's with comprehensive liability immunity.

Operational Governance and Repository Management
To ensure studio-wide compliance, all developers operating under the ZFG Studio's brand must adhere to the following management protocols:

Repository Isolation: All proprietary game logic and private server-side configurations must remain in private, restricted repositories. Public distribution of studio-proprietary code is strictly prohibited.

Branding Consistency: Every public repository must include clear attribution, identifying ZFG Studio's as the primary author and maintainer.

Liability Mitigation: All public utility modules must contain an explicit disclaimer of warranty to protect the studio from operational damages incurred by third-party implementations.

Version Control: Professional standards require the use of consistent versioning tags for all utility releases to indicate stability and developmental progress to the broader community.

The continued development of ZFG Studio's projects remains contingent upon the rigorous application of these standards. Any deviation from this licensing model requires executive approval.
